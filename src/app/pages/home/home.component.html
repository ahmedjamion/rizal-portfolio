Life, Works and Writings of Rizal

Portfolio

By: Ahmed Rashad I. Jamion

José Rizal was a remarkable Filipino whose life, works, and writings played a key role in shaping the nation's history. Through his powerful words and strong beliefs, he inspired change and left a lasting impact on Filipino society. This portfolio offers a glimpse into his legacy and the ideas that continue to influence people today.

One way Rizal's legacy is preserved is through Republic Act No. 1425, commonly known as the Rizal Law. Enacted in 1956, this law mandates the teaching of Rizal's life, works, and writings—especially Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo—in all schools across the Philippines. It aims to instill a sense of nationalism, critical thinking, and appreciation for the sacrifices made for the country’s freedom. Through this law, generations of students continue to learn from Rizal’s ideals and carry forward his vision for a better nation.
